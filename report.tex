%% ===== 1. DATASET =====
\section{Dataset and Signal Description}

\subsection{Raw Data}
The input is a 4.5\,GB CSV with 2{,}740{,}000 earnings-call observations from January 2010 to April 2026. Each row represents one transcript segment with 609 columns covering identifiers (GVKEY, BESTTICKER), timing (MOSTIMPORTANTDATEUTC), the headline signal (ATCClassifierScore $\in [-1,+1]$), 545 AspectTheme$\times$score NLP columns, and metadata (SECTOR, speaker type, call length).

\subsection{ATCClassifierScore}
The signal aggregates sentiment across all AspectTheme scores. Mean = 0.019 (management optimism bias), Std = 0.035, Spearman IC vs 5d returns $\approx 0.030$, vs 20d returns $\approx 0.055$.

\begin{callout}
\textbf{Critical:} \texttt{INGESTDATEUTC} $-$ \texttt{MOSTIMPORTANTDATEUTC} = 4{,}833 days mean. This is a \emph{batch export timestamp}, not per-call availability. Using it as a gate would block all pre-2023 trades. All entry timing uses \texttt{MOSTIMPORTANTDATEUTC} only.
\end{callout}

%% ===== 2. UNIVERSE =====
\section{Universe Construction and Point-in-Time Data}

\subsection{S\&P 500/400/600 via WRDS Compustat}
Source: \texttt{comp.idxcst\_his} (institutional WRDS subscription). Schema: (\texttt{gvkey}, \texttt{gvkeyx}, \texttt{from}, \texttt{thru}) --- exact entry/exit intervals. We auto-detect index GVKEYs by constituent count (SP500 $\approx 503$, SP400 $\approx 400$, SP600 $\approx 602$) and use vectorised GVKEY interval joins instead of BESTTICKER (which changes on corporate renames: Meta was FB, Twitter was TWTR).

\textit{Caveat:} Only current members (\texttt{thru=NULL}) returned by this subscription. Deleted companies absent, causing mild survivorship bias for pre-2020 periods ($<$2\% of events/year). Documented in audit.

\subsection{Russell 3000 --- Genuine Point-in-Time}
Built from \texttt{crsp.msf} (CRSP monthly market cap) at each last-Friday-of-June annual reconstitution. Top 3{,}000 US common stocks (shrcd~$\in$~\{10,11\}, exchcd~$\in$~\{1,2,3\}, prc~$>$~\$1). \textbf{No survivorship bias} --- a stock that peaked in 2015 correctly appears only in 2015's cohort. 16 recons, 47{,}901 interval rows, 6{,}626 unique GVKEYs.

%% ===== 6. NB05 FAILURE =====
\section{Walk-Forward LightGBM --- NB05 (Why It Failed)}

\begin{figure}[H]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.9\linewidth]{ic_baseline_vs_lgbm}
\caption{IC comparison: ATCClassifierScore (blue) vs LightGBM ensemble (orange). LightGBM underperforms in the 2020--2026 test period due to three compounding causes.}
\end{figure}

Three root causes explain the NB05 net Sharpe of $-0.538$ for SP500:

\textbf{(1) Regime change.} The expanding training window learns 2010--2019 patterns.
Mean IC at 5d: 0.060 (training) $\to$ 0.001 (test). The model overfits to a regime that no longer exists.

\textbf{(2) Noisy 5-day target.} As shown in the L/S decomposition, the short leg has Sharpe $= -0.76$ for SP500 at 5d. LightGBM correlates with ATCClassifierScore and inherits this short-leg failure.

\textbf{(3) Transaction cost structure.}
At IC $\approx 0.001$ and weekly rebalancing:
\[ \text{Gross alpha} \approx 0.001 \times \sqrt{52} \times 0.02 \approx 14\,\text{bps/yr} \ll \text{TC} \approx 520\,\text{bps/yr} \]

%% ===== 7. NB08 =====
\section{Regime-Aware XGBoost-DART --- NB08}

\textbf{DART} (Dropout Additive Regression Trees) randomly drops existing trees during boosting. This prevents over-reliance on any single tree --- crucial for sparse NLP signals with idiosyncratic patterns. The regime confidence gate:
\[ \hat{y}^{\text{regime}}_t = \hat{y}^{\text{XGB}}_t \times \sigma\!\left(\frac{\text{IC}_{12m} - \text{IC}^*}{\sigma_\text{IC}}\right) \]
When rolling IC $\approx 0$ (post-2021 for SP500), confidence $\to 0$ and positions shrink.

\begin{figure}[H]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.9\linewidth]{regime_confidence_timeline}
\caption{Rolling IC and regime confidence. The gate suppresses trading during 2021--2022 when earnings sentiment had near-zero predictive power for large caps.}
\end{figure}

\end{figure}

%% ===== 8. NB09 =====
\section{Improved LightGBM --- NB09 (Three Surgical Fixes)}

\subsection{Why Each Fix Was Needed}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
\textbf{Problem in NB05} & \textbf{Symptom} & \textbf{Fix in NB09}\\
\midrule
Expanding window trains on old regime & Model doesn't generalise post-2020 & Rolling 3-year window\\
Raw 5d return target is noisy & Short leg Sharpe $= -0.76$ & Rank of fwd\_20d\\
520\,bps/yr TC with IC $\approx 0$ & Guaranteed net loss & Monthly rebalance (120\,bps)\\
50 unstable features via MI & Fold-to-fold feature instability & Top-20 by IC stability\\
24 quarterly refits (slow) & 40+ minutes runtime & 6 annual refits (fast)\\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\textbf{Rank target:} $y_\text{rank} = \text{rank}(\text{fwd}_{20d}) / N$ within sector$\times$month. This transforms the target to uniform $[0,1]$, removing fat-tail outlier returns that overwhelm gradient updates and forcing the model to learn \emph{relative ordering} --- exactly what a long/short portfolio needs.

\textbf{Rolling 3-year window:} The model trained for 2023 sees data from 2020--2022 (post-COVID regime), not 2010--2019 (irrelevant bull-market era). This single change is responsible for the IC recovery in 2021: $-0.019$ (raw signal) $\to$ +0.068 (NB09) because the model learns that CFO-specific language and guidance acceleration remain informative even when the aggregate ATC score is noisy.

\textbf{IC-stability feature selection:} Features ranked by $\frac{\text{median IC}}{\text{std IC} + 0.01}$ --- stable signal over many years beats strong signal in one year.

%% ===== 9. NB10 =====
\section{Improved XGBoost-DART --- NB10}

\subsection{Same Three Fixes, Different Architecture}

NB10 applies the same rolling window, rank target, and monthly rebalance fixes to XGBoost-DART. Additional tweak: \texttt{rate\_drop=0.15} (vs NB08's 0.10) for stronger regularisation suited to the uniform rank target. \texttt{tree\_method=hist} and \texttt{n\_estimators=100} make it 8$\times$ faster than NB08.

\subsection{Why XGBoost-DART Excels at RU3K (1.108 vs 0.540 LightGBM)}

\begin{callout}
\textbf{Small-cap noise requires stronger regularisation.} RU3K contains $\sim$3{,}000 stocks including many sub-\$1B market-cap names with sparse NLP coverage (shorter calls, fewer analyst questions). LightGBM's greedy gradient updates can memorise these idiosyncratic patterns. DART's 15\% tree dropout discards any single tree's contribution to the prediction, forcing the ensemble to learn \emph{generalizable} cross-stock relationships that hold for many names simultaneously. Year 2024 IC: XGB = +0.108 vs LGBM = +0.075 --- DART's ensemble diversity provides higher IC in noisy markets.
\end{callout}

\textbf{Why SP500 favours LightGBM:} Large-cap names have denser, more consistent NLP coverage (longer calls, more analyst questions, less idiosyncratic noise). LightGBM's more aggressive gradient updates efficiently capture the subtle interactions between ATCClassifierScore, qoq\_delta, and sector\_pct\_rank that are stable across large-cap names. DART's random dropout discards some of these valid and stable patterns.

%% ===== 13. CONCLUSIONS =====
\section{Key Findings and Conclusions}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{The ATC signal is real and persistent.} Mean IC $\approx 0.030$--0.055 across 2010--2026, positive in 12/16 years, survives 5\,bps/side TC. Placebo test (Fluff/Filler) produces L/S Sharpe $\approx 0$.

  \item \textbf{Horizon matters more than model complexity for large caps.} The 5-day short leg fails (Short Sharpe $= -0.76$) due to momentum contamination. Extending to 20 days recovers the short-side alpha without any ML. This single insight is the most actionable finding.

  \item \textbf{Regime change explains original ML failures.} NB05/NB08 used expanding windows trained on 2010--2019 (IC $\approx 0.06$) and deployed in 2020--2026 (IC $\approx 0.001$). Rolling 3-year windows fix this, delivering +1.21 Sharpe improvement for SP500.

  \item \textbf{Rank target outperforms raw return target.} Cross-sectional rank within sector$\times$month removes outlier returns, creates uniform label distribution, and forces models to learn relative ordering --- delivering better generalisation.

  \item \textbf{Model-universe fit matters.} SP500: LightGBM (dense signal, stable patterns). SP1500: Regime-gated XGB (mid-cap IC variability requires explicit gating). RU3K: DART (small-cap noise requires strong regularisation). One model does not fit all universes.
\end{enumerate}
